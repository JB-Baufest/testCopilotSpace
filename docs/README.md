# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

This folder is the onboarding entry point for OctoAcme's project management process. It summarizes how projects move from idea to delivery and where to find the detailed guidance.

## Project management overview
OctoAcme uses a customer-first, iterative delivery model with clear ownership across PM, Product, Engineering, and QA, supported by shared artifacts such as one-pagers, backlogs, risk registers, and retrospectives.  
See: [Project Management Overview](./octoacme-project-management-overview.md)

## Project initiation
Projects begin with validation of business need, success metrics, stakeholders, initial risks, and a go/no-go decision to enter planning.  
See: [Project Initiation Guide](./octoacme-project-initiation.md)

## Project planning
Approved initiatives are broken into shippable backlog increments with acceptance criteria, estimates, dependencies, milestones, and Definition of Done.  
See: [Project Planning](./octoacme-project-planning.md)

## Execution & tracking
Delivery follows a regular team rhythm (standups, syncs, demos), board-based workflow, PR quality expectations, and metrics tracking with escalation paths for blockers.  
See: [Execution & Tracking](./octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md)

## Risk management & communication
Risks are managed through a maintained risk register (impact, likelihood, owner, mitigation, status) and consistent stakeholder communication using shared templates and escalation paths.  
See: [Risk Management & Communication](./octoacme-risks-and-communication.md)

## Release & deployment
Releases require acceptance completion, CI/security checks, release notes, rollback planning, staged deployment, post-deploy verification, and stakeholder announcement.  
See: [Release & Deployment Guide](./octoacme-release-and-deployment.md)

## Retrospective & continuous improvement
After sprints, releases, or incidents, teams capture what worked, what to improve, and track a small set of owned action items to drive continuous improvement.  
See: [Retrospective & Continuous Improvement](./octoacme-retrospective-and-continuous-improvement.md)

## Roles/personas
The process is supported by defined personas (Developers, Product Managers, Project Managers) with clear responsibilities, goals, and communication patterns.  
See: [Roles and Personas](./octoacme-roles-and-personas.md)
