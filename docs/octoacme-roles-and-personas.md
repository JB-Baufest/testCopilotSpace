# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Scrum Masters

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters help the team run an effective delivery rhythm. They coach agile practices, remove blockers, and protect focus so commitments are realistic and sustainable.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate standups, planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Surface and remove blockers with PM, PdM, and engineering leads
- Coach the team on agile working agreements and continuous improvement
- Track delivery flow health (carryover, cycle time, bottlenecks)

### Goals
- Keep delivery predictable and transparent
- Improve team effectiveness and collaboration quality
- Reduce recurring process friction

### Typical Communication
- Ceremony facilitation notes and action items
- Blocker escalation updates with owners and due dates
- Retrospective outcomes and follow-up tracking

### Typical Interaction Patterns
- Partners with **Project Managers** on planning cadence, risk escalation, and dependency visibility
- Works with **Developers** and **QA/Testing** on flow bottlenecks and Definition of Done adherence
- Aligns with **Product Managers** to keep backlog readiness and sprint goals realistic

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers turn product goals into usable experiences. They validate user needs, shape interaction design, and ensure delivered solutions are understandable and accessible.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and synthesize findings
- Create wireframes, flows, and prototypes for key scenarios
- Define UX acceptance criteria with Product and Engineering
- Run usability checks and recommend design improvements

### Goals
- Improve task success, usability, and user confidence
- Reduce rework caused by unclear user flows
- Keep design decisions evidence-based and consistent

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product, Engineering, and QA/Testing
- Prototype walkthroughs and feedback summaries
- UX decision notes linked to backlog items

### Typical Interaction Patterns
- Collaborates with **Product Managers** to refine problem statements and define user outcomes
- Partners with **Developers** to ensure implementation fidelity and feasible scope
- Supports **Stakeholder Proxy** in capturing feedback from user groups not present day-to-day

---

## Data Analysts

### Role Summary
Data Analysts help teams make evidence-based decisions. They define metrics, produce analyses, and translate results into actionable recommendations.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain product and delivery KPIs
- Build dashboards and perform ad hoc analysis
- Validate hypothesis outcomes after releases
- Identify trends, risks, and opportunities from data

### Goals
- Improve decision quality with reliable evidence
- Shorten time from question to insight
- Increase visibility into business and delivery impact

### Typical Communication
- KPI dashboard updates and analysis summaries
- Experiment/readout reports with recommendations
- Data-quality assumptions and caveats documentation

### Typical Interaction Patterns
- Works with **Product Managers** to define success metrics and evaluate outcomes
- Supports **Project Managers** with delivery health and risk indicators
- Provides **Stakeholders** and **Stakeholder Proxy** with transparent progress and impact reporting

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Stakeholder Proxy

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Proxy represents stakeholder groups that are not continuously present in team ceremonies. This role gathers input, clarifies intent, and keeps decisions aligned with business context.

### Responsibilities
- Consolidate feedback from distributed stakeholder groups
- Clarify stakeholder priorities and non-functional expectations
- Escalate unresolved decision points to the right owner
- Confirm decision outcomes are communicated back to stakeholders

### Goals
- Reduce ambiguity in stakeholder expectations
- Speed up decisions when direct stakeholder access is limited
- Maintain trust through consistent feedback loops

### Typical Communication
- Consolidated feedback briefs before planning/review meetings
- Decision request summaries with business context and urgency
- Outcome recap notes shared with stakeholder groups

### Typical Interaction Patterns
- Aligns with **Product Managers** on prioritization trade-offs and acceptance expectations
- Coordinates with **Project Managers** on timing, dependencies, and communication plans
- Collaborates with **UX Designers** and **Data Analysts** to ground stakeholder requests in user evidence and measurable outcomes

---

## Cross-role accountability highlights
- **Product Managers** are accountable for *what/why* (outcomes, prioritization, success metrics).
- **Project Managers** are accountable for *when/how coordination* (delivery planning, risks, dependencies, status).
- **Scrum Masters** are accountable for *team process health* (cadence, blockers, continuous improvement).
- **UX Designers** are accountable for *experience quality* (user flows, usability, accessibility).
- **Data Analysts** are accountable for *measurement quality* (KPIs, analysis, insight reporting).
- **Developers** are accountable for *implementation quality* (technical delivery, maintainability, testability).
- **Stakeholder Proxy** is accountable for *stakeholder signal quality* (feedback synthesis and expectation alignment).
